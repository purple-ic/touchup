Third-party integration

TouchUp does not require authorization for basic usage. However, additional features become available if you choose to log into third-party services.
TouchUp stores third-party authorization tokens locally on your device. These tokens and any information you share with TouchUp are only shared with relevant services, and only for relevant functions:
  • If you've logged in to YouTube through TouchUp, your Google token is stored on your device until you log out through the application or manually delete the file.
    This token is only ever transferred to official Google APIs and is only used whenever you attempt to upload to YouTube, or if you try to log out of YouTube (TouchUp automatically revokes your active token if possible).
    Google's own privacy policy applies to any data you submit on their websites when logging in, and any information you provide through our YouTube upload feature.
    The YouTube authorization file stored on your device may be deleted manually by clicking "Open data folder" in TouchUp and deleting the yt_tokencache.json file (alternatively yt_tokencache) through your file viewer.
    Your YouTube authorization information is not saved to file locally if you turn off "Stay logged in to YouTube" in the app's settings.
Third party services may collect personal information and may perform tracking. Please read their respective privacy policies.

Personal information

TouchUp does not transmit any information (personal or otherwise) outside of your own device except for Third-party integration.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy occasionally.
Each version of the privacy policy applies specifically to certain versions of TouchUp, which are listed at the top of this version of the privacy policy. Updates will generally only involve changes to this section or the addition of more applicable TouchUp versions.
