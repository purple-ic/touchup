Privacy policy - version 1

Updated on 14/08/2024, created on 08/08/2024 (DD/MM/YYYY)
Applies to: TouchUp version 0.1.0

Third-party integration

TouchUp does not require authorization for basic usage. However, additional features become available if you choose to log into third-party services.
TouchUp stores third-party authorization tokens locally on your device. These tokens and any information you share with TouchUp are only shared with relevant services, and only for relevant functions:

YouTube

If you've logged in to YouTube through TouchUp, your Google token is stored on your device until you log out through the application.
This token is only transferred to official Google APIs and is only used for uploading to YouTube, or for revoking itself (TouchUp automatically revokes your token when you log out through the application).
TouchUp does not access nor collect any Google/YouTube user data, it simply allows you to upload videos.
Google's own privacy policy applies to any data you submit on their websites when logging in, and any information you provide through our YouTube upload feature.
Your YouTube authorization information is not saved to file locally if you turn off "Stay logged in to YouTube" in the app's settings.

If your token is saved on your device, then it is saved to your device's credential manager (which differs depending on your OS). All requests involving your token are securely encrypted with HTTPS.

Personal information

TouchUp itself does not collect any information (personal or otherwise).
If you make use of any Third-party integrations (such as YouTube), they may collect your information. Please see their respective privacy policies.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy occasionally.
Each version of the privacy policy applies specifically to certain versions of TouchUp, which are listed at the top of this version of the privacy policy. Updates will generally only involve changes to this section or the addition of more applicable TouchUp versions.
